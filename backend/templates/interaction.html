Continuous authentication systems enhance security by continuously verifying the identity of a user throughout an active session. Unlike traditional authentication methods that rely only on passwords or one-time verification, continuous authentication ensures that the person using the system remains the same from login to logout. This is achieved by analysing behavioural biometrics such as typing patterns, keystroke dynamics, and mouse movements. Each individual has a unique way of interacting with a computer, including how long they press keys, the time between key presses, typing speed, and the rhythm of their input. Similarly, mouse behaviour such as movement speed, direction changes, and activity levels also contribute to identifying a user.
